Detailed Service Actions

1. Adoption Process

Our adoption services are tailored for responsible individuals aged 18 or older who are looking for a true long-term commitment to providing animals with safe and loving homes. To pursue this action, you must first meet the animal in person, complete an application form containing your ID and proof of residence, follow a scheduled home inspection, and once everything is officially approved, sign your contract and collect your new pet.

2. Donations Program

Donations are open to anyone and directly cover the operational costs of food, blankets, life-saving surgeries, and general maintenance of our shelter sites. To pursue this action, you select your desired method of payment and specify where the money should flow towards, such as kennels or surgeries. If you have any physical items you are willing to donate such as clothes or toys, please drop them off directly at your nearest branch.

3. Reports Department

Reports can be generated by any person who witnesses animal abuse, neglect, endangerment, or encounters stray animals, helping us directly save the lives of animals. To pursue this action, locate your nearest branch and safely document the scene with photos or videos, making sure to add the exact time and address. Contact the local or national branch for immediate emergencies, or send an email for non-urgent records. You can report completely confidentially and provide a formal written statement.
